Initial Dose: 10 to 15 mg/kg/dose IV every 6 to 18 hours, with adjustments based on PMA and postnatal age.

PMA: Postmenstrual age is gestational age plus postnatal age. It is the primary determinant of dosing intervals, with postnatal age as a secondary qualifier.

Administration

  • Administer via intermittent IV infusion over 60 to 120 minutes.
  • Max rate: 10 mg per minute, not exceeding 5 mg/mL concentration. Up to 10 mg/mL for fluid-restricted patients.

Uses

Vancomycin is primarily used for treating serious infections caused by methicillin-resistant staphylococci and penicillin-resistant pneumococci.

Common uses include:

  • Systemic anthrax (when meningitis can be ruled out)
  • Clostridium difficile infection
  • Infective endocarditis

Special Considerations

  • Renal function should be monitored closely, and dosing adjusted accordingly.
  • Drug elimination correlates with PMA, affecting dosing intervals.

Adverse Effects

  • Nephrotoxicity and ototoxicity, especially with concurrent aminoglycoside use.
  • Red Man Syndrome: rash and hypotension. Lengthening infusion time usually helps.
  • Neutropenia with prolonged use.

Monitoring

  • Auditory function should be monitored to prevent ototoxicity.
  • Monitor renal function to minimize nephrotoxicity risks.
  • Trough levels should be checked to maintain efficacy and avoid toxicity.

Vancomycin Concentration

Trough Concentrations: Should be obtained just before the next dose. Target troughs are around 10 mg/L (range 7‚Äì15 mg/L).

Solution Compatibility

Compatible solutions include D5W, D10W, and Normal Saline (NS).

Terminal injection site compatibility: Amikacin, ampicillin, calcium gluconate, insulin, and many more. Incompatible with: cefazolin, cefepime, piperacillin, and others.
